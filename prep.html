Healthiest Ingredients - from Real Simple

  • Mushrooms: Meaty and filling, as a stand-in for beef they can slash up to 400 calories from a meal. They may also protect against breast cancer by helping to regulate a woman’s estrogen levels.

  • Walnuts: A surprisingly good source of omega-3 fatty acids. Those are the fats that lower the bad-for-you cholesterol (LDL) and raise the good-for-you kind (HDL).

  • Peanut Butter (All-Natural): Heart-healthy monounsaturated fats abound in these protein-rich spreads. Opt for those with just two ingredients—nuts and salt.

  • Almonds: Packed with monounsaturated fatty acids, which keep blood vessels healthy. The plant fibers help lower cholesterol.

  • Blueberries: Packed with fiber, this superfruit was one of the top antioxidant-rich picks in a U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA) study.

Chicken Stir-Fry

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup carrots
  • 1/2 cup celery
  • 1/4 cup onion
  • 1 green pepper
  • 1 tablespoon garlic
  • 1 tablespoon ginger
  • 1 pound chicken
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 3 cups cooked white rice
  1. Heat a large pan with vegetable oil over high heat
  2. Add vegetables and garlic to the hot pan. Stir until garlic is slightly brown. If using ginger, add that too.
  3. Add the chicken and sugar to the pan. If using soy sauce, add that too.
  4. Stir the food for about 1 minute in the pan.
  5. Serve with cooked rice.

Tuna Apple Salad Sandwich

  • 1 apple
  • 1 can 12-oz chunk light tuna
  • 2 tablespoons low-fat plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons reduced-fat mayonnaise
  • 1/2 cup raisins
  • 1/4 cup chopped walnuts
  • 1/8 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/2 teaspoon curry powder
  • 8 leaves lettuce
  • 8 slices whole-grain bread
  1. Cut apple in quarters; remove core and chop.
  2. In a medium size bowl, mix all salad ingredients, except tuna.
  3. Gently fold in tuna.
  4. Make sandwiches, using lettuce and whole-grain bread, and fill with tuna apple salad.
